## **INT. ABANDONED ATRIUM – LATE AFTERNOON**

Sunlight spills through broken skylights, carving slow-moving lattices across the marble floor.  
The building breathes—creaks, dust motes, the faint hum of wind.

**VAL** kneels in the middle of it all, sleeves rolled, a small **BONSAI TREE** before her.  
A chipped teacup of water.  
Drip. Drip. Drip.

Each pour is careful, meditative. The kind of motion made by someone who finally has time.

From the far end of the atrium—  
FOOTSTEPS.

A silhouette crosses through a rib of light.  
**AELLA**, cigarette hanging from her mouth, coat torn at the collar, hair wind-tangled.

She pauses when she sees her sister.  
The scene is so absurd it borders on holy.

A long drag. The ember glows, fades.  
Aella exhales smoke that twists in the gold light.

She lingers in the threshold, weight shifting, eyes narrowing in disbelief—  
but something about the stillness catches her.  
She stops mocking it in her head. Just… watches.

Val doesn’t turn. She knows who it is.

Aella flicks her cigarette to the side, grinds it out on a loose tile.  
Then, quietly—

She walks forward.  
Boots scuffing marble.  
Each step a soft echo in the empty hall.

She stops a few feet away.  
Val glances up—half a smile, faint but genuine.

Aella hesitates. Then lowers herself down beside her—awkwardly, carefully, like she’s afraid the peace might spook and run.  
The marble is cold against her palms. She sits cross-legged, mirroring Val without realizing it.

For a while, they just exist.  
Water. Breath. Wind.

Val tips the teacup again.  
Tiny droplets darken the soil.

Aella leans back on her hands, tilts her face toward the sun.  
A moment passes—quiet, warm, surreal.

**AELLA**  
(softly)  
So… you gonna introduce me?

Val chuckles under her breath.  
Sets the cup down.

**VAL**  
This is Ash.  
Don’t ask me why—it just felt right.

**AELLA**  
Ash, huh?  
Figures. Everything around us ends up that way.

A beat. Then, softly—

**VAL**  
Not everything.

Aella looks at her. There’s no edge in Val’s tone—just calm, surprising calm.  
She studies her sister’s face. Finds something unrecognizable there. Peace.

**AELLA**  
You’ve changed.

**VAL**  
Maybe I just stopped running the lights.

**AELLA**  
That’s one way to kill an audience.

Val smiles—genuine this time.  
She dips her finger into the water, lets it fall drop by drop onto a leaf.

**VAL**  
Sometimes I wonder…  
If it’d be better off out there.  
In real soil.  
Sunlight. Rain.  
Maybe this pot’s just a cage we painted pretty.

**AELLA**  
You talking about the tree or yourself?

Val exhales a quiet laugh, but her eyes don’t leave the bonsai.

**VAL**  
Does it matter?  
I used to think I was made for the crowd—the hum, the screens, the spectacle.  
And when that vanished…  
_(beat)_  
I realized I’d been living in a pot too.  
Shaped to fit what people needed me to be.

Aella leans forward, elbows on knees, listening.  
No snark. Just her sister, honest for once.

**VAL (CONT'D)**  
If I plant it outside, maybe it’ll die.  
If I keep it here, maybe it survives…  
but never knows the sky.

She traces a finger along a branch.  
The leaf trembles.

**AELLA**  
Maybe it’s not about the pot.  
Maybe it’s about who keeps showing up to water it.

Val looks at her. The words land gently, like sunlight on wet soil.

**VAL**  
You sound almost wise.

**AELLA**  
Don’t tell anyone. I’ve got a reputation.

They share a small laugh.  
Val slides the cup toward her.

**VAL**  
Here. Your turn.

Aella eyes the cup like it’s radioactive.

**AELLA**  
You trust me with this thing?

**VAL**  
You’ve handled worse disasters.

Aella takes the cup, tips it carefully.  
A few drops miss the mark, splash the marble.

She mutters something, embarrassed. Val grins.

The two sisters sit together, framed in the ruinous light—  
a god and her mirror, tending a tiny fragment of life.

The outside world rumbles faintly—distant thunder, or maybe machines dying.  
It doesn’t touch them.

**AELLA**  
You ever think you’ll go back?

**VAL**  
To what?  
_(beat)_  
The noise? The spotlight?  
No. I think… I like hearing my own thoughts.  
Even the ugly ones.

Aella studies her. Then looks at the bonsai.

**AELLA**  
You really named it Ash?

**VAL**  
Yeah.  
_(smiles)_  
Because that’s what you build from.

Aella nods—quietly, sincerely.

They sit there.  
No more dialogue, no music.  
Just the light dimming, the air cooling, the small tree breathing between them.

For the first time in their long lives—  
they’re not gods, not legends, not weapons.

Just sisters.  
And the sound of water finding soil.
